Charades

A Warm Smile

A mask.

An uplifting laugh

An anguished cry.

The facade of strength

A blanket thrown over the dampened pillow.

Sticks and stones break the bones

But words hurt just as much,

Sometimes to think before we speak

Heals more than a gentle touch.

Walking home from school with friends

But still-somehow-all alone,

Wearing the ruse of celebration

Yet mournful hearts within.

This game of charades continuing on

Through every day of life.

The contentment on faces

Dont show sleepless nights

Smiling actors and actresses

Dont hint of secret suffering.

Come and join this game with me,

This guessing game,

This dancing game

Come and try to lift the mask,

Find the lock or the key buried too deeply

In this game

-Of charades.
